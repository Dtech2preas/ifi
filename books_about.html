About Platform

This platform is a student-centered platform that makes buying and selling textbooks simple and convenient. Instead of sifting through scattered group chats, students can explore all available textbooks in one organized space. Each listing provides clear book details, pricing, and direct contact information, enabling fast, easy connections between buyers and sellers. Our goal is to save time, reduce frustration, and make the experience smooth for all students.

The platform is open to all students and encourages peer-to-peer interaction. Whether you’re selling old textbooks or looking for the next semester’s materials, the marketplace is designed to make the process straightforward, transparent, and reliable.

Jonas Mochebane (also known online as PREASX24) is a South African Independent Software Architect, CEO, and digital builder. He is the visionary founder and driving force behind The D-TECH Ecosystem — a growing, interconnected network of practical, community-oriented digital platforms.

This ecosystem includes multiple projects engineered to support learners and young creators:

  • www.preasx24.co.za – The official gateway and map for the entire D-TECH Ecosystem.
  • grade12.dtech-services.co.za – High-school resources, past papers, notes, quizzes, and university guidance.
  • books.dtech-services.co.za – Open student marketplace for buying and selling textbooks.
  • student.dtech-services.co.za – Work-in-progress student support platform for financial assistance.
  • apps.dtech-services.co.za – Collection of free, lightweight apps designed for students and general users.

All projects are architected and maintained by a single independent developer, Jonas Mochebane, demonstrating that enterprise-grade, highly accessible technology can be built on a solo budget through extreme technical resourcefulness.

Through this ecosystem, Platform strives to bridge the gap between education, technology, and student needs, offering free and easy-to-use resources that help learners succeed academically and access tools that would otherwise require costly subscriptions.
